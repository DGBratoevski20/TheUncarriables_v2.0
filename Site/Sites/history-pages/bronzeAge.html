Farming has spread across Europe.

Farmers from the Middle East headed toward Europe, seeking new land to cultivate. They traveled either along the Mediterranean coast or the Danube River, encountering hunter-gatherers who lived in dense forests.

First evidence of habitation at Thebes.

The city of Thebes was founded by Kadmos. He was Phoenican.

3000 BCE

First ziggurats in Ur, Eridu, Uruk, and Nippur.

The Mesopotamian civilization built the first ziggurat.

2100 BCE

Domesticated horses introduced in Mesopotamia.

Indo-Europeans brought the horse to the Near East.

2000 BCE

Bronze working introduced to Egypt.

Egyptians used bronze to create tools, weapons, statues, jewellery, etc.

1800 BCE

The site of Delphi is first settled.

Delphi lies between two towering rocks of Mt. Parnassus, known as the Phaidriades (Shining) Rocks, in the Regional unit of Phocis in Central Greece

1500 BCE

Mycenaean Epidaurus at its peak of prosperity.

The Mycenaeans extended their influence throughout the Peloponnese in Greece and across the Aegean from Crete to the Cycladic islands. They are named after their chief city of Mycenae in the Argolid of the northeast Peloponnese.
