The Italian poet Dante Alighieri completes his epic the Divine Comedy.

Alighieri finished the poem a year before his death in 1321.

1320 AD

The Italian poet and scholar Petrarch rediscovers Cicero's 'lost' Letters to Atticus in Verona.

The letters weren't previously known to have existed.

1345 AD

Giovanni Boccaccio completes his masterpiece, the Decameron.

The book is structured as a frame story containing 100 tales told by a group of seven young women and three young men; they shelter in a secluded villa just outside Florence in order to escape the Black Death, which was afflicting the city.

1353 AD

Giovanni Boccaccio works on his Ancestry of the Pagan Gods (Genealogia Deorum Gentilium).

The book is an ambitious work of humanistic scholarship whose goal is to plunder ancient and medieval literary sources so as to create a massive synthesis of Greek and Roman mythology.

1360 AD

Sir Thomas More's 'Utopia' is published.

The book was first published in Belgium.
