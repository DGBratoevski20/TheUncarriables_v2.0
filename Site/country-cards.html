European Union

Austria


Austria is located in Central Europe. It is divided into nine states, with Vienna as the capital. The official language is German, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Bulgaria


Bulgaria has a strategic geographic position as a bridge between Europe and Asia. Bulgaria is divided into 28 provinces, with Sofia as its capital. The official language of Bulgaria is Bulgarian, and the currency is the lev (BGN).

Belgium


Belgium lies in the heart of the European Union (EU). It has a population of about 11 million people and three official languages: Dutch, French, and German. The currency of Belgium is the euro (EUR).

Croatia


Croatia is divided into 20 counties and the city of Zagreb, which is also the capital of Croatia. The official language of Croatia is Croatian, and the currency is the kuna (HRK).

Republic of Cyprus


The Republic of Cyprus is a European Union (EU) island member state in the Eastern Mediterranean Sea. Cyprus adopted the euro (EUR) as its national currency on 1 January 2008. Nicosia is the capital city

Czech Republic


The Czech Republic is a landlocked country in Central Europe. It is divided into 14 regions, with Prague as the capital. The official language of the Czech Republic is Czech, and the currency is the Czech koruna (CZK).

Denmark


Denmark is a Scandinavian country located in Northern Europe. It is divided into five main regions, with Copenhagen as its capital. The official language of Denmark is Danish, and the currency is the Danish krone (DKK).

Estonia


Estonia is located in Northeastern Europe. It is divided into 15 counties and 79 local municipalities, with Tallinn as its capital city. Estonian is the official language of Estonia, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Finland


Finland, a Nordic country in Northern Europe. It is divided into five regions, with Helsinki as the capital. The official languages of Finland are Finnish and Swedish, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

France


France is a founding member of the European Union (EU). It is divided into 12 metropolitan regions, with Paris as the capital. The official language of France is French, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Germany


Germany is located in Central Europe. It is divided into 16 provinces, and its capital is Berlin. The official language of Germany is German, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Greece


Greece is a parliamentary republic along the Mediterranean Sea in Southeastern Europe. The capital of Greece is Athens, the official language is Greek, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Hungary


Hungary, a landlocked country in Central Europe, is split into 19 counties, with Budapest as its capital. The official language of Hungary is Hungarian, and the currency is the Hungarian forint (HUF).

Ireland


The island of Ireland is situated in Northwest Europe and is approximately 20 kilometres from Great Britain at its nearest point.. Its official languages are Irish and English and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Italy


Italy is located along the Mediterranean Sea in Southern Europe. After the Second World War, in 1946, a popular referendum abolished the monarchy and proclaimed Italy a parliamentary republic. The currency is the euro (EUR)

Latvia


Latvia, a country located in Northern Europe. Latvia is divided into 110 municipalities and nine cities, with Riga as the capital. The national language is Latvian, and the national currency is (EUR).

Lithuania


Lithuania is located in Eastern Europe along the Baltic Sea. The capital of Lithuania is Vilnius. The official language is Lithuanian, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Luxembourg


Luxembourg, a landlocked country in Western Europe, is bordered by France, Belgium, and Germany. The official languages of Luxembourg are Luxembourgish, French, and German. In 1999, Luxembourg adopted the euro (EUR) as its currency.

Malta


Malta is an archipelago located in the Mediterranean. In 1974, Malta was declared a Republic. Malta has two official languages: Maltese and English, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Netherlands


The Netherlands is a constituent country located in Western Europe and with territories in the Caribbean. It is bordered by Belgium and Germany. Netherlands is a member of the European Union and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Poland


Poland is situated in Central Europe. Poland is divided into 16 provinces (voivodeships), with Warsaw as the capital. The official language is Polish, and the currency is the zloty (PLN).

Portugal


Portugal, on the Iberian Peninsula in Southwestern Europe. It is divided into 18 districts, with Lisbon as the capital. The official language of Portugal is Portuguese, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Romania


Romania is located at the crossroads of Central and Southeastern Europe. The capital and largest city is Bucharest. The official language is Romanian, and the currency is the Romanian leu (RON).

Slovakia


The Slovak Republic (Slovakia) is a landlocked country in Central Europe. Its capital is Bratislava. The official language of the Slovak Republic is Slovak, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Slovenia


Slovenia, a country in Central Europe. It is subdivided into 212 municipalities, with Ljubljana as the capital. The official language of Slovenia is Slovene, and the currency is the euro (EUR).

Spain


Spain, located in Southwest Europe on the Iberian Peninsula, is divided into 17 comunidades autonomas and two autonomous cities, with Madrid as the capital. The official language of Spain is Spanish. The official currency is the euro (EUR).

Sweden


Sweden is situated on the Scandinavian Peninsula in the north of Europe near the Atlantic Ocean and the Gulf Stream. Stockholm, the capital, is on the same latitude as the southern tip of Greenland. Swedish is the official language and the currency is the krona (SEK).
